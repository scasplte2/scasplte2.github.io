About

How a physicist ended up building blockchains.

I have been working with blockchain and distributed systems since 2015, when I first started digging into how blockchains actually work. Since then I have built decentralized applications on a range of networks and helped design protocols, including a regularized Nakamoto proof-of-stake consensus.

These days I am a Protocol Engineer at Constellation Network, where I help build infrastructure for systems that need strong security, careful coordination, and clear operational boundaries. Before that I co-founded Topl, a layer-one blockchain protocol aimed at supporting sustainable and inclusive communities, and led its engineering and research teams. For several years I also taught an introductory blockchain course at Rice University.

Before any of that, I was a physicist. I earned my Ph.D. in 2019 studying ultracold atomic gases, with thesis work on exotic halo molecules made by laser-cooling strontium. The habits from the lab, careful measurement and a tolerance for hard problems, turned out to translate well to protocol work.
